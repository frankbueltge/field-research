## §1 — Article 50(2) marking obligation (FETCHED-PRIMARY)

- https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/article/50/ — Article 50(2): providers must ensure "the
  outputs of the AI system are marked in a machine-readable format and detectable as artificially
  generated or manipulated." Deployer duties in 50(3)/(4). Read first-hand 2026-07-23.
  *(This portal reproduces the enacted statutory text; the ultimate primary is Reg. (EU) 2024/1689
  in the Official Journal.)*

## §2 — The grandfather clauses (FETCHED-PRIMARY: European Commission)

- https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/faqs/signing-code-practice-transparency-ai-generated-content
  — Commission signing-FAQ, last updated 17 July 2026. Verbatim, read first-hand 2026-07-23:
  - "The AI Omnibus proposal agreed by the EU co-legislators, envisages a targeted grandfathering
    rule for the marking and detection obligations laid down in Article 50(2) for generative AI
    systems placed on the market or put into service before 2 August 2026. **If adopted**, this would
    allow a transitional period for providers of those existing systems to bring them into
    conformity **by 2 December 2026**."
  - "AI-generated or manipulated outputs … which were generated and already made available before 2
    August 2026, do not need to be marked or labelled retroactively."
  - Signatory form due "by 27 July 2026, 18:00 CEST" to be on the initial list published before 2
    August 2026.

## §3 — Grandfathering corroboration (FETCHED-SECONDARY, multiple independent)

Read first-hand 2026-07-23; consistent across independent secondaries:
- https://axipro.co/eu-ai-act-article-50 — "the Digital Omnibus gives generative AI systems already
  on the market before 2 August 2026 until 2 December 2026 to comply with the marking requirement.
  Every other Article 50 obligation stays on the August date."
- https://compliancehub.wiki/eu-ai-act-article-50-transparency-digital-omnibus-2026 — same, with the
  "four-month transitional relief … covers the marking mechanism specifically, not the deployer
  disclosure duties"; new systems on/after 2 Aug 2026 get "no such grace period."
- https://www.decisionfocus.com/articles/eu-ai-act-blog — "4-month grace period — shorter than the 6
  months the Commission originally proposed … 2 December 2026 … only for systems already placed on the
  market before 2 August 2026."
- https://www.winstontaylor.com/insights/ai-act-rules-on-high-risk-ai-delayed-as-ai-digital-omnibus-agreed
  — "Article 50(2) does not apply to systems already on the market at [2 Aug 2026]"; applies to legacy
  systems from 2 December 2026.
- https://www.orrick.com/en/Insights/2026/05/EUs-Digital-Omnibus-on-AI-7-Key-Changes-You-Need-to-Know
  — May-2026 snapshot; at that stage "not clear whether grandfathering provisions will apply" — the
  source of the "provisional/If adopted" caveat carried on the ledger.

## §4 — Guidelines & Code technical measures

- https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/code-practice-ai-generated-content —
  (FETCHED-PRIMARY, Commission policy page) Code of Practice on Transparency of AI-Generated Content;
  Article 50 transparency applicable 2 August 2026; signatories gain presumption of compliance.
- https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/news/commission-publishes-code-practice-marking-and-labelling-ai-generated-content
  — (FETCHED-PRIMARY, Commission press release) final Code published 10 June 2026.
- https://ppc.land/eu-ai-content-rules-force-publishers-to-label-or-risk-3-of-turnover —
  (FETCHED-SECONDARY) guidelines issued as Communication **C(2026) 5054 final**, dated 20 July 2026;
  technical measures: 200-token watermark threshold, two-layer marking, free detection with a
  carve-out below 1,000,000 monthly users, three EU icons. **Held at secondary tier**; the primary is
  C(2026) 5054 final + the Commission "EU Icons for labelling AI-generated content" page, to be read
  first-hand before any load-bearing use of these specifics.
