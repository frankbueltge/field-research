### Skeptic (convened, pre-build read) — verdict: SURVIVES WITH CONDITIONS

Eight objections, three high-severity. All eight applied to `data.json` before the Builder was
briefed:

1. **[HIGH] The "validity condition" was invented** — "dual disclosure suffices to inform" is
   nowhere in the Guidance; refuting a design intent the collective wrote for the standard is a
   strawman. *Applied:* the conductor retrieved the Guidance's §7.4 purpose section; the work now
   quotes the standard's own stated benefit ("transparency for stakeholders") and states the
   collective's evaluative test as the collective's own, explicitly not the standard's.
2. **[HIGH] The 38.4× ratio manufactures drama** — the market-based residual is *designed* to
   shrink toward zero as procurement succeeds, so the ratio grows mechanically; alone it reads as
   proof of gaming. *Applied:* every ratio on the work is paired with the absolute gap; the
   derivation-formulas block carries the mechanical-growth disclosure verbatim; the finding says
   a large ratio "is not itself an indictment."
3. **[HIGH] "Smaller or shrinking" flattened Google's rising figure** — Google's market-based
   Scope 2 rose +235.6% over the window; the disjunction invited a false parallel with
   Microsoft's decline, the same display-layer sleight the work indicts. *Applied:* split into
   two exhibits — Microsoft = meter choice (direction reversal), Google = window choice layered
   on meter choice (an 11% year-over-year reduction headlined on a meter up 235.6% over five
   years). The Skeptic's framing ("a second, distinct discretion") is now the Google exhibit.
4. **[MEDIUM] AI causation asserted in the thesis, conceded in a footnote.** *Applied:* verb
   downgraded to "coincides with"; the conjecture flag stays attached to the finding.
5. **[MEDIUM] "AS METERED" overclaims** — only the kWh is metered; the emissions figure is
   modeled. *Applied:* METER B renamed "AS THE GRID SAW IT"; each invoice carries a subline
   distinguishing metered consumption from the modeled grid-average factor, on its face.
6. **[MEDIUM] Year misalignment must live on the exhibit.** *Applied:* each invoice carries its
   company's year-basis line on its face, not only in the caveats.
7. **[LOW] Sole-source arithmetic.** *Applied:* an audit strip on the work shows the formulas and
   worked examples so a reader can check the arithmetic from the invoice itself.
8. **[LOW, Skeptic's own conjecture] The market-based headline may be institutionally required,
   not merely chosen.** *Checked against the primary:* substantiated for Google — its carbon goal
   is defined on the market-based meter and SBTi-validated (February 2025, per its own report).
   *Applied:* the goal-anchor quote and a caveat state that the meter choice is institutionalized
   in an external validation ecosystem; the work explicitly does not claim mere optics.
   The Skeptic's structural note (n=2, not an industry rate) is a caveat and a finding-point
   qualifier ("in these two examined cases").
