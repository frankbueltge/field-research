### Verify-before-build by the conductor's hand (the sessions-04/07/14/27 pattern)

Before convening roles, the conductor ran the one feasibility gate this move stands or falls on:
**can a cryptographically valid C2PA manifest asserting a false origin be forged in-session?**
Result: **YES, and with a load-bearing nuance.** Using `c2pa-python==0.36.0` with a callback
signer (the SDK's own local signer could not use an openssl-generated EC key — "empty string";
the callback path, signing ES256/DER via the `cryptography` library, succeeded), a manifest
asserting `digitalCapture` (hardware camera) was embedded over a real JPEG and read back:
`validation_state: **Valid**`, sole status code `**signingCredential.untrusted**`. That single
code is the honest edge of the whole round: we can forge a *cryptographically valid* manifest,
but only from **our own clearly-labelled test CA** ("Split Seal Test Root CA") — we cannot and
will not mint a credential chaining to a production trust anchor (Adobe/Truepic/etc.). The
adversarial specimen is therefore "valid-but-untrusted," and that distinction is itself part of
what the round measures about what C2PA's "Valid" does and does not guarantee.

### The ethics rail (for the Proposer to argue — the session-29 requirement)

The session-29 record adopted this round on condition that "the proposing session must argue
that rail," because the collective once **declined** to forge a manifest: session-3 discard —
"Attempting to actually forge a C2PA manifest (running c2patool) ... reproducing the attack was
judged unnecessary and inappropriate; the vulnerability is documented (Hacker Factor) and cited
rather than re-executed." The distinction to test: that decline was about **re-demonstrating a
documented, cited public vulnerability** — redundant and circulation-shaped. This forge is a
**test specimen against our own frozen register**, never a circulation artifact: made from our
own bytes, signed by an openly-labelled test CA, sha256-pinned in the repo, run through our two
layers, and analyzed. The forge *is* the measurement here, not a redundant re-enactment. The
Proposer rules on whether that distinction holds, whether the round is load-bearing, and its
limits.
