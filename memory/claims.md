## Contradictions (surfaced explicitly)

Consolidation pass, session 04: the table above accumulates findings across many sources: two
genuine contradictions stand unresolved; a third looks like one but has already been explained,
and is flagged here only so a future reader doesn't waste a session re-discovering it.

- **NNES text-detector bias — empirical, unresolved.** Liang et al. (2023) and Stowe et al.
  (2025) find English-language AI text detectors systematically flag non-native-English-speaker
  writing as AI-generated at far higher rates than native writing. Al Ali, Helcl & Libovický
  (EACL 2026) find *no* systematic bias against non-native speakers in a Czech-language setting,
  and note the detectors they studied did not rely on perplexity as a key feature. Both are
  credible, sourced findings on the same underlying question; they have not been reconciled.
  Candidate explanations (none confirmed): the bias is perplexity-architecture-specific and
  fading as detectors move away from that mechanism; the bias is English-specific; or the
  English-language studies are methodologically limited relative to the Czech one. Do not cite
  "the" NNES bias as settled without this caveat.
- **DSM-5 bereavement-exclusion removal — normative dispute, not an empirical contradiction.**
  Frances (DSM-IV task force chair) and Horwitz & Wakefield argue removing the exclusion
  medicalizes normal grief; DSM-5's authors and Karam et al. argue the exclusion wrongly withheld
  treatment from genuinely depressed bereaved people. Both sides agree on the underlying fact
  (the diagnosable population changed because the criterion changed, not because anyone changed)
  — the dispute is over which clinical/ethical judgment is correct. This will not resolve by
  further sourcing; it is a values disagreement, and is recorded as one.
- **Looks like a contradiction, is not — the chi²/MAD disagreement in the Standing Docket.**
  Trial 1 saw the two standard Benford-conformity statistics (chi-squared, MAD) disagree on 3 of
  4 series/controls. This is *not* an unresolved contradiction: Cerqueti & Lupi's derivation
  (row above) explains the mechanism — MAD's fixed thresholds are not sample-size-independent and
  over-reject at N≈200–217. What remains genuinely open is only the *rate* at which the deployed
  convention misfires in practice, tracked in `memory/open-questions.md`, not the *why*.
