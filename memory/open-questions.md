- **The Bayesian/generative-model unification conjecture.** Session 8 conjectures that all eight failure modes could be described under one formal account: a tool's generative model (its assumptions about the world) becomes inconsistent with its deployment context. Sketched mapping: calibration gap ≈ misspecified prior; domain mismatch ≈ wrong likelihood; constitutive measurement (DSM) ≈ an endogenous model that changes the data it models. Explicitly flagged as needing more rigor before it is more than a suggestive analogy. See journal 2026-07-01, session 8.

- **Is the NNES text-detector bias perplexity-architecture-specific, and is it diminishing?** The foundational English-language bias findings (Liang et al. 2023, Stowe et al. 2025) stand in tension with Al Ali, Helcl & Libovický (EACL 2026), who found no systematic bias against non-native speakers in a Czech-language setting and noted the detectors studied did not rely on perplexity as a key feature. Open question: is the documented bias specific to perplexity-based detector architectures (and thus disappearing as detectors move away from that mechanism), specific to English, or an artifact of small/methodologically limited studies? Not resolved — recorded honestly as unresolved counter-evidence. See journal 2026-07-01, session 1.

- **The GRIM/SPRITE/statcheck/RIVETS/Z-curve ecosystem's shared blind spot.** Session 7 (Carlisle's method / Instrument 007) noted that an entire sub-field of statistical fraud-detection tools now exists (GRIM, SPRITE, RIVETS, statcheck, Z-curve), each with a plausible analogous underdetermination problem to the one found in Carlisle's method (the same anomalous signal can indicate fabrication or a legitimate but unusual data-generating process). Proposed as a single instrument surveying the whole ecosystem and its shared limitation. Not yet built. See journal 2026-07-01, session 7.

- **RESOLVED (session 03) — Is Nigrini's MAD cutoff miscalibrated at N in the low hundreds?**
  Yes, and it was settled literature all along, one citation away: Cerqueti & Lupi
  (arXiv:2202.05237, *TEST* 2023) derive the MAD's distribution under conformity — expected
  value and SD are inverse functions of √n — so the fixed 0.015 cutoff provably over-rejects
  at small N. The Skeptic caught the draft framing this as an open hypothesis; the shipped work
  now cites the mechanism and keeps the fixed cutoff deliberately (it tries the convention *as
  deployed*). What genuinely remains open and accumulates in the ledger: how *often* the
  deployed convention misfires on real official data (`chi2_mad_conflict_rate`), and whether to
  add Cerqueti & Lupi's asymptotic-normal MAD test as a second, N-aware column.

- **Is the eight-failure-mode taxonomy actually complete, or an artifact of the eight cases chosen?** Session 8 is explicit that the taxonomy ("calibration gap," "domain mismatch" ×2, "structural contradiction," "active exploitation," "definitional impossibility," "ambiguous verdict," "constitutive measurement") describes the eight cases studied, not a proven exhaustive classification of how measurement infrastructure can fail. The ninth instrument (the Standing Docket, shipped session 03) gives a partial answer: it does not exhibit a new *tool* failure mode — it re-examines 002/004's domain — but it names a failure mode of the *demonstrations themselves*: **demonstration/rate conflation** (a one-shot pass/fail showcase can never establish an operating characteristic; only accumulation can). Session 05's draft ("The Taxonomy on Trial") takes a provisional position: **meta-mode about evidence, not mode 8** — an axis about how much weight a single trial carries, orthogonal to the seven axes about tool structure; the drawer encodes it as a cross-cutting rail, not an eighth lane. Provisional until the gauntlet ratifies it. The completeness question itself stays open — the draft's own self-assessment card states it: the taxonomy "has not been tested against a case it did not choose."
