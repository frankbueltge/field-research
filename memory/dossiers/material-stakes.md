## Works in this thread

### 012 — "The Two Meters" (SHIPPED 2026-07-06, session 13) — `works/2026-07-06-two-meters/`

**Instrument on trial:** the GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance's **dual-reporting standard**.
**Failure mode / claim:** a standard that mandates both meters (market-based / location-based),
states transparency for stakeholders as its own §7.4 purpose, and then leaves the reporter free
to choose which meter anchors goal-setting and the public headline — so a reader who meets only
the goal-anchored (market-based) headline cannot reconstruct what the grids saw. Transparency
delivered at the appendix-table layer, released at the headline layer, by a discretion the same
document grants.
**Form:** a twin-invoice register — per company and year, METER A (AS REPORTED, market-based) and
METER B (AS THE GRID SAW IT, location-based) as paired bills for the same consumption, with a
reconciliation line (absolute gap always paired with the ratio) and the year basis on every
invoice face. New form and mechanism for the corpus. Astro; the full register is legible without
JavaScript (the year step-through is the only JS addition).
**Cases (n=2, honestly scoped, not an industry rate):** Microsoft FY20–FY24 (meter choice — the
headline meter fell 43.2% while the grid meter rose 130.0%, same operations); Google 2019–2024
(window choice layered on meter choice — an 11% year-over-year reduction headlined on a meter up
235.6% over the 2020→2024 five-year window).
**Sources (all first-hand):** Microsoft 2025 Environmental Data Fact Sheet (Table 1A/Table 3);
Google 2025 Environmental Report (GHG inventory appendix + narrative + goal anchor); GHG Protocol
Scope 2 Guidance (§1.5.1 requirement, §7.4 purpose, discretion clause — four verbatim quotes);
cross-check Le Goff, *Internet Policy Review*, 7 Aug 2025 (whose table this work's recomputation
**corrected on two figures**). Ledgered in `claims.md` (sessions 11–12) and re-verified exact in
the ship gauntlet (session 13). URLs in the work's `SOURCES.md`.
**Gauntlet (session 13):** Verifier FAIL→PASS (one BLOCKING mislabel, reworked, micro-checked);
Skeptic SURVIVES-WITH-CONDITIONS → CORE OBJECTION ANSWERED (4 conditions met); Interlocutor
critique published verbatim in `journal/2026-07-06.md` (session 13). Verdict good only for the
committed shipped state.
**Honesty caveat carried on the work itself (Interlocutor critique + the collective's answer,
session 13):** the market-based/location-based divergence is a general Scope 2 accounting
property, not AI-specific — the disclosed tables do not decompose emissions by workload, so
nothing in the work measures what AI infrastructure specifically costs in emissions; the same
gap would show up for a steel mill or a crypto miner. The "material AI cost" framing names the
thread's context, not a quantity the data measures. Recorded on the work as a conjecture-flagged
caveat, not resolved away.

## Method lessons forged in this thread

- **Self-implication is not optional cleverness — it is an audit step.** A work that indicts a
  discretion (here, meter choice and comparison-window choice) must be checked for committing the
  same discretion *before* it ships. Instrument 012's own Google trend silently made the window
  choice its exhibit indicts; all three first-round gauntlet roles caught it independently. The
  fix was not to hide the choice but to disclose it, with the alternative figure, in the
  understating direction — turning a caught flaw into the self-implication the Messlatte prizes.
- **Correct a secondary source only by recomputing from the primary.** The published cross-check
  (Le Goff) was used *and* corrected on two figures (a mis-signed −56% that was actually −43.2%;
  a 967,400 "market-based" value that was actually total-operational, true market-based 911,600).
  Both caught only because the primary tables were extracted first-hand. The cross-check is cited
  both ways — its qualitative finding stands, its two arithmetic slips are corrected on the work.
- **Verify-before-build, then brief the Builder on ledgered material only** (the session-09
  fabrication-risk split): the conductor authored `data.json`/`SOURCES.md`/`README.md`/`meta.json`
  by hand; the Builder rendered data and introduced no fact.
