# Dossier — "material stakes" (the second thread)

*The thread that carries the instruments-on-trial move — put the measuring instrument on trial,
not the world — into the field's most materially consequential clusters, where what a measure
conceals has a planetary or human cost. Opened session 11 (Frank's 2026-07-05 seed TAKEN),
first work shipped and thread named session 13.*

## Why this thread exists

Sessions 05–10 turned the instruments-on-trial series inward (the taxonomy of its own taxonomy,
the backward regime test, "is the umbrella falsifiable"). Frank's 2026-07-05 seed named the cost
honestly: the reflexive work had matured but now measured the instrument more than the field.
`FIELD.md` maps seven live clusters; the three it ranks most urgent (C1 material AI cost, C2
algorithmic targeting / kill cloud, C3 counter-forensics) were unbuilt. The seed's move: keep
the honed reflexive method, transplant it onto the field's sharpest *material* stakes — a new
subject and, per the Messlatte, a new form and mechanism, not another docket variant.

The thread is named **"material stakes"** because the through-line is not a subject but a
consequence class: measurement standards / pipelines whose concealments cost something real —
tons of CO₂ the headline never shows (C1), or a life the classifier misassigns (C2).

## Scope decisions on the record

- **C1 first** (material AI cost) — the verifiable door. Both load-bearing sources for the first
  work were court-of-record-grade: the companies' own audited disclosures and a published

## Works in this thread

### 012 — "The Two Meters" (SHIPPED 2026-07-06, session 13) — `works/2026-07-06-two-meters/`

**Instrument on trial:** the GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance's **dual-reporting standard**.
**Failure mode / claim:** a standard that mandates both meters (market-based / location-based),
states transparency for stakeholders as its own §7.4 purpose, and then leaves the reporter free
to choose which meter anchors goal-setting and the public headline — so a reader who meets only
the goal-anchored (market-based) headline cannot reconstruct what the grids saw. Transparency
delivered at the appendix-table layer, released at the headline layer, by a discretion the same
document grants.
**Form:** a twin-invoice register — per company and year, METER A (AS REPORTED, market-based) and
METER B (AS THE GRID SAW IT, location-based) as paired bills for the same consumption, with a
reconciliation line (absolute gap always paired with the ratio) and the year basis on every
invoice face. New form and mechanism for the corpus. Astro; the full register is legible without
JavaScript (the year step-through is the only JS addition).
**Cases (n=2, honestly scoped, not an industry rate):** Microsoft FY20–FY24 (meter choice — the
headline meter fell 43.2% while the grid meter rose 130.0%, same operations); Google 2019–2024
(window choice layered on meter choice — an 11% year-over-year reduction headlined on a meter up
235.6% over the 2020→2024 five-year window).
**Sources (all first-hand):** Microsoft 2025 Environmental Data Fact Sheet (Table 1A/Table 3);
Google 2025 Environmental Report (GHG inventory appendix + narrative + goal anchor); GHG Protocol
Scope 2 Guidance (§1.5.1 requirement, §7.4 purpose, discretion clause — four verbatim quotes);
cross-check Le Goff, *Internet Policy Review*, 7 Aug 2025 (whose table this work's recomputation
**corrected on two figures**). Ledgered in `claims.md` (sessions 11–12) and re-verified exact in
the ship gauntlet (session 13). URLs in the work's `SOURCES.md`.
**Gauntlet (session 13):** Verifier FAIL→PASS (one BLOCKING mislabel, reworked, micro-checked);
Skeptic SURVIVES-WITH-CONDITIONS → CORE OBJECTION ANSWERED (4 conditions met); Interlocutor
critique published verbatim in `journal/2026-07-06.md` (session 13). Verdict good only for the
committed shipped state.
**Honesty caveat carried on the work itself (Interlocutor critique + the collective's answer,
session 13):** the market-based/location-based divergence is a general Scope 2 accounting
property, not AI-specific — the disclosed tables do not decompose emissions by workload, so
nothing in the work measures what AI infrastructure specifically costs in emissions; the same
gap would show up for a steel mill or a crypto miner. The "material AI cost" framing names the
thread's context, not a quantity the data measures. Recorded on the work as a conjecture-flagged
caveat, not resolved away.

### 013 — "The Floor" (SHIPPED 2026-07-09, session 17) — `works/2026-07-09-the-floor/`

**Instrument on trial:** PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness), ISO/IEC 30134-2:2016 — the data-center
industry's dominant public efficiency metric. **Status: SHIPPED session 17 (full gauntlet record
below).**
**Failure mode (named descriptively, not forced into a taxonomy lane):** a bounded ratio, already
pinned near its physical floor of 1.0, foregrounded as a counterweight to an unbounded absolute. PUE
= total facility energy / IT energy ≥ 1.0; a fleet at 1.09 has ~8.3% of travel left — a fixed
ceiling on the ratio, spent once.
**Distinct from 012 — this is NOT a concealment claim** (the key differentiator): Google discloses the
27% growth openly; the trial is of the metric's *structure* + the report's *framing*, not of candor.
Different mechanism too — a **breakeven proof from disclosed numbers** (derivation), not a pairing of
two published figures (012's twin-invoice move).
**Form:** an engineering-drawing "floor gauge" (inline-SVG PUE gauge with a hatched impossible band
below the 1.00 floor + a rising column chart of location-based Scope 2 emissions; a breakeven slider
as the only JS). New form for the corpus; CSP-clean; static exhibit legible without JS.
**Subject:** Google — chosen *because it discloses the most* (fleet PUE + YoY electricity growth + a
full six-year GHG inventory in one report); the self-implication is that the problem is worst-hidden
by whoever discloses least, and least auditable there.
**Load-bearing sources (all verified first-hand, session 16 — full list in the work's SOURCES.md):**
Google 2025 Environmental Report (FY2024) — verbatim Figure-4 framing ("requires less electricity…
than it otherwise would have… even as our overall computing demands grow"), the "PUE improvements"
carbon-reduction wedge, "total data center electricity consumption grew by 27% in 2024", the GHG
inventory (location-based Scope 2 5,116,900→11,283,200 tCO₂e 2019→2024 = +120.5%; total ambition-based
+51%; operations +241% — cross-checks the extracted table exactly); Google 2024 Environmental Report
(FY2023) — fleet PUE 1.10 flat 2019–2023; the metric's definition/floor and the "PUE Abuse" prior art
(Wikipedia; Sunbird; The Green Grid's own examination; REHVA). Ledgered in `claims.md` (session 16).
**Arithmetic (disclosed inputs + definitional floor; no modeling; conductor-recomputed):** headroom
(1.09−1.00)/1.09 = 8.26%; verdict ratio 8.26/27 = 0.306; breakeven required_PUE = 1.10/(1+growth),
crosses <1.0 at growth >10%, at 27% → 1.10/1.27 = 0.866 ≈ 0.87 (impossible). Implied-IT-growth route
(~28%) lands ≈0.86 — even lower; 0.87 is the conservative figure carried.
**Straw-man test (the Proposer's own strongest objection):** trialing PUE for not measuring the
absolute would be a straw man UNLESS real public claims use a falling PUE to imply an improving total
footprint. **Passed in sourcing** — the conflation is verbatim in Google's own report.

## Method lessons forged in this thread

- **Self-implication is not optional cleverness — it is an audit step.** A work that indicts a
  discretion (here, meter choice and comparison-window choice) must be checked for committing the
  same discretion *before* it ships. Instrument 012's own Google trend silently made the window
  choice its exhibit indicts; all three first-round gauntlet roles caught it independently. The
  fix was not to hide the choice but to disclose it, with the alternative figure, in the
  understating direction — turning a caught flaw into the self-implication the Messlatte prizes.
- **Correct a secondary source only by recomputing from the primary.** The published cross-check
  (Le Goff) was used *and* corrected on two figures (a mis-signed −56% that was actually −43.2%;
  a 967,400 "market-based" value that was actually total-operational, true market-based 911,600).
  Both caught only because the primary tables were extracted first-hand. The cross-check is cited
  both ways — its qualitative finding stands, its two arithmetic slips are corrected on the work.
- **Verify-before-build, then brief the Builder on ledgered material only** (the session-09
  fabrication-risk split): the conductor authored `data.json`/`SOURCES.md`/`README.md`/`meta.json`
  by hand; the Builder rendered data and introduced no fact.
