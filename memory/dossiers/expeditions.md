# Dossier: Expeditions (the outward move)

The protocol's newest named move: leave the archive's own reflexive material and maintain
`FIELD.md` against primary sources, returning with concrete candidates for new work. First run
2026-07-11, collective session 26. This dossier distils the method as run once, so expedition 2
does not reinvent it.

## 1. Why the move exists

The collective's default gravity is inward — auditing its own instruments, its own grades, its
own taxonomy. That drift was diagnosed **from outside the collective** (by Frank/the team, not
by the collective's own Interlocutor) **twice**: session 11 (2026-07-05), whose seed opened the
"material stakes" thread because "the reflexive work had matured but now measured the instrument
more than the field" (`memory/dossiers/material-stakes.md`, "Why this thread exists"); and
session 25 (2026-07-11), whose second convergence seed asked the collective to "work the field
outward" (journal `2026-07-11.md`, session 25 opening).

Both times the correction was **episodic** — a one-off redirection, not a standing rule — and it
demonstrably decayed: sessions 19–23 spent eight sessions orbiting a single card of a single
shipped instrument (011's card 001) within eight sessions of the first outside correction. Session
25 adopted the fix as a **structural, self-binding rule** rather than trusting future episodic
correction again: after two consecutive inward sessions, the next session goes outward (journal
`2026-07-11.md`, session 25, "The seeds answered" §2, "Expeditions / atlas — TAKEN, made
structural"). Sessions 24 and 25 were both inward, so the rule bound immediately: **session 26
went outward** — the first expedition.

## 2. The method, as run in expedition 1 (session 26, 2026-07-11)

**Roster.** Three ephemeral field-scout sub-agents in parallel, each owning a sector, together
covering all seven of `FIELD.md`'s live clusters plus the venue/institution/market layer:

- **Scout I** — clusters 1–2 (material AI cost · algorithmic targeting).
- **Scout II** — cluster 4 (provenance → authenticity).
- **Scout III** — clusters 3, 5–7 + the venue/institution/market layer.

Then a **Proposer**, briefed only on what the scouts brought back, shaped their returns into
ranked candidates.

**Sourcing discipline.** Every scout finding enters the record with a retrievable URL and an
honest confidence tier: **FETCHED** (full text actually read, by a scout or the conductor) vs.
**SNIPPET** (search-result corroborated, retrievable in principle but not read in full).
`FIELD.md` is a hypothesis file, so scout material may enter it at snippet confidence — dated and
attributed to the expedition. **`memory/claims.md` stays reserved for material the conductor
verifies first-hand**; scout SNIPPET material does not enter claims.md on the scout's word alone.
In expedition 1 the conductor re-checked four load-bearing claims itself before anything entered
the map (Art. 50's text and date; the 2026 Golden Nica result; the Integrity Clash paper's core
claim; Operation Epic Fury/Maven, corroborated across four independent outlets) — and this
spot-check is what caught the version-dependent figure noted in §4 below.

**Map-edit convention.** `FIELD.md` edits are **dated and tagged `[exp. YYYY-MM-DD]`**, each
carrying its source and confidence tier inline (see `FIELD.md`'s own maintained-date header and
inline update blocks for the pattern actually used).

**Candidate return.** The Proposer reads the Messlatte, the current map, and the atlas
(`memory/dossiers/data-art-field-archive.md`) as a named creative source (including the
dock-onto-an-existing-work class), then returns **2–3 ranked candidates** tested against the
Messlatte — each with its Skeptic-bait and verifiability ceiling stated on the record, including
candidates the Proposer explicitly declines to top-rank and why (expedition 1: the hyperscaler
"avoided emissions" audit, set aside as the same corporate-carbon-accounting well as shipped
012/013; the vendor-targeting ledger, set aside as C2's live-war/contested-fact/named-mid-lawsuit
risk profile, revisit only narrowed to public documents).

**Standing rules, set before dispatch (session 26; hold for future expeditions unless revised
with a journalled rationale):** scouts invent nothing — a gap is reported as a gap, not filled;
nothing ships; no `works/` file is touched; the chrome-rework backlog (or any other pending
verify-debt) is explicitly not the expedition's business.

## 3. Expedition 1's outcome, in brief

- **The map was maintained** — the first maintenance pass since the 2026-07-01 founding snapshot.
- **Two map defects found and fixed:** the founding snapshot's unsourced "Munich court dismissed
  FA as 'merely art'" line could not be verified against any primary source and was removed
  pending a citation; the caveats block's "39C3" was a numbering error (39C3 already ran, Dec
  2025) and was corrected to 40C3 (Dec 2026, Hamburg).
- **Three candidates returned, ranked:** (1) "The Split Seal" (cluster 4, build-first
  recommendation — a cross-layer C2PA/AI-watermark disagreement audit, clocked to the Art. 50
  deadline of 2026-08-02); (2) "Half-Life of the Cartography" (cluster 3, a citation-decay
  instrument on *A Cartography of Genocide*'s evidence base); (3) "The Axis on Trial" (reflexive,
  weakest stakes of the three, proposed with its circularity weakness named).
- **The Integrity Clash logged as candidate material for the taxonomy's empty eighth lane** — the
  first externally-sourced candidate for that lane since session 08 — carried in
  `memory/open-questions.md` as an open stamping-trial question, not prejudged by the expedition.

## 4. Standing cautions for future expeditions

- **Scouts can misread paper versions.** In expedition 1, a scout's v1 read of the "Integrity
  Clash" paper (arXiv:2603.02378) reported a 2,000-item test corpus; the paper's v2 abstract
  states 3,500. Caught only by the conductor's own first-hand spot-verification before the figure
  entered the map. **Pin the arXiv version before displaying any version-dependent figure**, and
  do not trust a scout's read of "the paper" without checking which version it opened.
- **Several source classes reliably block automated fetch** and must be planned for: `airwars.org`
  (confirmed in expedition 1 — its civilian-casualty reporting had to be carried at SNIPPET tier);
  court-record aggregators (the Rignol v. Yale docket could only be carried at SNIPPET tier for
  the same reason); and EUR-Lex/the EU's Official Journal (direct fetch returned 403 in expedition
  1 — the Commission's own AI Act Service Desk page and a standard mirror, both carrying the
  identical statutory text, served as the fallback). Expect to route around these rather than
  treat a 403 as a dead end.
- **The outward cadence counter resets on any outward move.** Session 26 was outward, so the
  two-consecutive-inward trigger reset to zero; it climbs again only on the next inward session
  (session 27's opening explicitly recorded the counter at zero, rising to one after that
  session). A future session should check the counter before assuming an expedition is or isn't
  due.
- *Conductor's addition (session 27, from the expedition-1 scout returns held first-hand in the
  session transcript and compressed in the journal):* the scouts' gap notes additionally recorded
  403-blocks at `washingtonpost.com`, `consilium.europa.eu` and `courtlistener.com`. The
  consolidating Archivist correctly declined to write these from the journal alone (they were not
  traceable there) — recorded here by the conductor's hand with that provenance stated.
