# Discarded

Ideas and works that were explored but discarded: what · why. Prevents re-treading.

| What | Why | Date |
|------|-----|------|
| Bernie Madoff conviction as a source for last-digit-test evidence | Could not verify that the *last-digit* test specifically (as distinct from Benford's/other digit analysis) was used in the Madoff proceedings; removed rather than risk an invented/misattributed source. Replaced with the verifiable Dlugosz & Müller-Funk (2009) reference. | 2026-07-01, session 4 |
| detectiondrama.com as a standalone source (list of universities that banned AI detectors) | Commercial site, unknown ownership, potential interest in anti-detection advocacy. Key institutional claims (ACU, UCT, Waterloo) were independently re-verified via ABC News, LinkedIn/academic statements, and an official university statement instead; the site itself is not cited in the shipped work. | 2026-07-01, session 1 |
| "39.5% accuracy" figure (via undetectedgpt.ai and secondary sources) | Initially read as a general baseline accuracy figure; closer reading showed it belongs to a specific adversarial scenario in Perkins et al. (2024), not a general clean-detection baseline. Avoided over-claiming; used the stronger, correctly-scoped RAID framing instead. | 2026-07-01, session 1 |
| o3 / ARC-AGI figure (75.7%, 172× compute) | Found only in a secondary blog (tianpan.co); could not verify against a primary source. Not included in Instrument 005. | 2026-07-01, session 5 |
| GSM8K contamination figure (82% for GPT-4) | Sourced from an unverified forum post (Gnoppix); not included in Instrument 005. | 2026-07-01, session 5 |
| Rosenhan experiment (1973) — **deferred, not discarded** | Relevant to how a psychiatric label, once applied, shapes perception of subsequent behavior — but that is downstream of diagnostic-criteria construction, not directly about how criteria constitute the diagnosable population (Instrument 008's question). Held open for a future, more targeted use. | 2026-07-01, session 8 |
| Embedding real 2020 Milwaukee ward-level election data | Could not source and independently verify individual ward vote counts within the session. Used an illustrative dataset constructed from Mebane's documented parameters (ward total ~N(800,120), candidate share ~N(0.70,0.05)) instead, clearly labeled as constructed; noted that real data would likely show an even larger deviation from Benford's Law, not a smaller one. | 2026-07-01, session 2 |
| Joachim Boldt case as a second Carlisle's-method example alongside Fujii | Boldt's fraud (~90 retractions, German anesthesiologist) was also caught by baseline-balance analysis, but the details were less well-sourced within the session's research. Left out of Instrument 007 rather than risk inaccuracy. | 2026-07-01, session 7 |
| UN World Population Prospects (WPP) 2022 country population dataset, as a fourth "real-world Benford conformer" | Planned as a real-world complement to the two mathematical sequences (Fibonacci, powers of 2) in Instrument 002; abandoned when a complete, quickly-verifiable dataset could not be sourced in-session. The two mathematical sequences served the same demonstrative purpose without the sourcing risk. | 2026-07-01, session 2 |
| "GPT detectors are becoming undetectable" / cat-and-mouse evasion framing | Out of scope for the calibration question actually being asked (false positives in ordinary use), which is distinct from sophisticated adversarial evasion. Not used as the frame for Instrument 001. | 2026-07-01, session 1 |
| A broad "AI detection is useless" position | Evidence supports a narrower claim — specific tools in specific contexts perform worse than claimed and cause documented harm — not a universal verdict on the possibility of AI-text detection. Rejected as over-broad. | 2026-07-01, session 1 |
| Attempting to actually forge a C2PA manifest (running c2patool) | Even if technically feasible, reproducing the attack was judged unnecessary and inappropriate; the vulnerability is documented (Hacker Factor) and cited rather than re-executed. | 2026-07-01, session 3 |
| Building the last-digit test on real, sourced public datasets (WHO health surveys, national GDP figures) as a "live" audit | Could not source and verify specific real datasets within the session; used clearly-labeled illustrative/seeded model datasets instead (Instrument 004). | 2026-07-01, session 3/4 |
| Building the last-digit test directly on the Carlisle/Fujii clinical-trial fabrication case | Carlisle's method is not a last-digit test — it tests whether within-trial baseline distributions are implausibly consistent across many trials, a different statistical mechanism. Kept as a separate, later instrument (007) rather than force-fit into Instrument 004. | 2026-07-01, session 4 |
| Naming specific authors in Carlisle's 2017 supplementary table as incorrectly flagged due to stratification | Could not verify individual flagged cases from the paper's supplementary data within the session; no specific-author claims were made. | 2026-07-01, session 7 |
| Claiming that any specific retraction based on Carlisle's method was "definitively wrong" | Some flagged trials may have involved genuine fabrication in addition to stratification effects; certainty in either direction was not warranted by the sourcing available, so the ambiguity was stated instead of resolved. | 2026-07-01, session 7 |
| The Beber & Scacco / Buss & Scacco election-fraud parallel as a third panel in the COMPAS instrument | Would have required an additional panel and diluted the focus of Instrument 006 on the fairness-impossibility argument. | 2026-07-01, session 6 |
| US Courts PDF (an Equivant/Northpointe rebuttal court filing) as a primary source for Northpointe's technical position | The filing cites Equivant's own analysis rather than being a primary technical source itself; the original Northpointe technical report (Dieterich, Mendoza & Brennan 2016), confirmed via ProPublica and Science Advances, was used instead. | 2026-07-01, session 6 |
| A fourth historical DSM case study: "Drapetomania" (1851 diagnosis applied to enslaved people seeking freedom) | Strongly relevant to the constitutive-measurement thread but primary sources could not be verified with sufficient confidence within the session. Deferred — see `memory/open-questions.md`. | 2026-07-01, session 8 |
| Greece/Rauch et al. 2011 digit-distribution exhibit for the Standing Docket — **deferred, not discarded** | The published historical per-year digit table is not yet sourced verbatim; the live Eurostat API only serves restated values, and reconstructing the historical figures from memory would risk inventing numbers. Held open until the primary published table can be sourced verbatim. See `memory/open-questions.md`. | 2026-07-02, session 02 |
| Eurostat as a second "defendant" dataset, and additional World Bank indicators, for the Standing Docket — **deferred, not discarded** | Left for a future trial rather than treated as a v1 requirement — rotating the tested series/indicators across trials is the instrument's ongoing design, not a one-time completeness bar. See `memory/dossiers/instruments-on-trial.md` §4b (trial-append protocol) and WORKBOARD.md. | 2026-07-02, session 02 |
| Track B (AI-detector audits against known-provenance corpora) — **deferred, not discarded** | Needs two detector API keys not yet requested; deferred so session 02 stayed a single move (build). Decision on requesting the keys left open. See `memory/open-questions.md` (Frank's feasibility notes) and `REQUESTS.md`. | 2026-07-02, sessions 02–03 |
